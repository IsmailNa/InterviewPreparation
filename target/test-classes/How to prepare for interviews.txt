Highlight your training and projects: Emphasize the knowledge and skills you gained during your training program and any practical projects you completed. Discuss the methodologies, tools, and technologies you learned, showcasing your understanding and ability to apply them in real-world scenarios.

Emphasize transferable skills: Identify transferable skills from your previous experiences that align with the IT field. For example, skills such as attention to detail, problem-solving, analytical thinking, communication, teamwork, and time management are valuable in IT roles. Showcase how these skills can be applied effectively in an IT environment.

Leverage personal projects and self-learning: If you worked on personal IT projects or engaged in self-learning outside of your formal training, highlight these experiences during the interview. Demonstrate your enthusiasm for the field and your ability to learn independently.

Showcase relevant certifications: If you have obtained any certifications related to manual or automation testing or cloud concepts, make sure to mention them. Certifications can demonstrate your dedication and commitment to expanding your knowledge and skills.

Create a strong resume: Craft a well-structured resume that highlights your training, projects, and relevant skills. Tailor your resume to the specific job you're applying for, focusing on the skills and experiences that closely match the requirements. Use action verbs and quantify your achievements whenever possible.

Practice interviewing: Prepare for the interview by practicing common interview questions and formulating thoughtful responses. Focus on showcasing your problem-solving abilities, technical knowledge, and adaptability. Consider conducting mock interviews with friends or seeking guidance from a career counselor to gain confidence and receive feedback.

Build a portfolio: Create a portfolio to showcase your practical work, including any projects you completed during your training or personal initiatives. A portfolio can provide tangible evidence of your abilities and allow you to demonstrate your skills during the interview.

Network and seek opportunities: Connect with professionals in the IT industry through networking events, online forums, or social media platforms. Attend industry conferences or webinars to stay updated on the latest trends and technologies. Networking can open doors to job opportunities and provide valuable insights from experienced professionals.

Remember, honesty is essential during the interview process. While you may not have professional experience, highlighting your training, projects, certifications, and transferable skills can demonstrate your readiness to contribute in an IT role. Stay confident, showcase your passion for learning, and emphasize your potential to grow and succeed in the field. Good luck with your interview!
